About Me

I am a First Class Honours graduate in Statistics, from the Federal University of Technology, Akure, Nigeria. My academic journey has given me a strong foundation in statistical theory, econometric methods, and applied data analysis using R, Python, and Stata.

I am particularly drawn to causal inference methods and their applications in economics and policy evaluation. My undergraduate thesis explored model misspecification in production function estimation, which sparked my interest in developing robust statistical methods that perform well under real-world conditions. I am currently exploring Bayesian approaches to causal inference and learning how these methods can address the challenges of policy evaluation in volatile economies.

I am actively seeking PhD opportunities where I can develop methodological expertise in causal inference and Bayesian statistics while working on economically meaningful applications. I believe that rigorous statistical methodology, combined with careful attention to real-world economic contexts, can lead to research that is both theoretically sound and practically relevant.
